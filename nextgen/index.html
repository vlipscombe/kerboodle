Next generation Kerboodle

We’re delighted to announce that Kerboodle, originally published by Nelson Thornes, is now part of Oxford University Press. We are committed to providing the highest quality support for Kerboodle, and as such, are making some exciting changes.

If you know and love Nelson Thornes Kerboodle, this page will help you to understand what these changes are, and what you need to do, so that you can continue to enjoy the very best teaching and learning support for your subject.

FAQs

Overview

Q: Why is Kerboodle moving?

A:

Technology moves on at a very fast pace, and it was time for Kerboodle to undergo a thorough revision, so that you can use it whenever and however you need to. We’ve taken on board what you’ve said you like, and what you don’t like, to improve Kerboodle in the best possible ways for teaching and learning.

Q: How is the new Kerboodle different from the old Kerboodle?

A:

  • Kerboodle has had a complete re-design, so will look and feel fresh and different.
  • New Kerboodle and almost all of its content can be viewed and used on a range of devices including tablets such as iPads - visit the Technical support pages for more information.
  • Most courses include a dedicated Markbook module, for tracking student assignments, logging their submissions, recording scores, and reporting on achievement. See the Assessment support pages for more information.
  • Kerboodle books are fully integrated with the rest of the course. Resources can be easily accessed and browsed from a button in the book. The book itself can be launched to exactly the right page from a number of different places in the course. See the Digital Book support pages for more information.
  • A single Administrator role manages the creation of all student accounts, so that your school data can be well maintained, and no student has more than one set of login details. See the Administrator support pages for more information.
  • Most licences for products on new Kerboodle will automatically be renewed, so you never have to worry about losing access. You will receive plenty of notice of this renewal, and always have the option to cancel.
  • Every course has a built-in course homepage, to give you updates and advice on your products, course and subject.
  • A new Kerboodle hub with all of the latest information about Kerboodle and available products can be accessed at www.oxfordsecondary.co.uk/kerboodle

Q: How do I get to the new Kerboodle?

A:

From 1st September 2014 new Kerboodle will be accessed at www.kerboodle.com. Until then, new Kerboodle can be found at kerboodle.oxfordsecondary.co.uk

Q: When will the new Kerboodle be available?

Call 01536 452620, email schools.enquiries.uk@oup.com or speak to your local Oxford Educational Consultant.

Q: Who should be the ‘purchasing teacher’?

A:

The purchasing teacher is the person who orders a course. This person will be contacted annually to authorise renewal of your course, and they will receive the initial access details. They should not be your intended Kerboodle administrator.

We recommend that the purchasing teacher should be a teacher within the faculty who will be using a course.

But we will be in touch with you to discuss the changes, and you are free to cancel if you wish. We will also be getting in touch if your course is not going to be available from September.

Logging in and User management

Q: Can I use my old login details? (Username/password/institution)

A:

We wanted to be certain that your data is accurate and secure. For this reason, you will need to set up your accounts again in the brand new Kerboodle site. This will ensure that you only have accounts that you need, and that all of the information is correct..

This is very easy and straightforward to do. This Getting started guide will take you through the steps.

Teachers in new Kerboodle will each require a unique email address. This allows you to make use of the Forgotten Password facility. We recommend using your school email address. Students have the option of adding their email address to make use of this function.

Q: Will I need to setup my students again?

A:

Your students will need new accounts creating for them. This is to ensure that when you start using new Kerboodle, each student only has one set of login details, and has access to the correct institution and courses. It also means that any redundant accounts will be removed.

Adding students is a very straightforward process. It is handled by the school’s single new Kerboodle Administrator (we recommend a data manager, or IT technician, if possible) and is a simple spreadsheet upload. This Getting started guide for the Administrator will take you through the steps. You can find out more about the Administrator on the Administrator help pages.

From September teachers will be able to give students access to the courses, and the correct groups, by creating group codes, just like in current Kerboodle.

Q: Will Frog SSO be available for Kerboodle in September?

A:

Frog single sign on will no longer be available for Kerboodle from September. But you could easily add links between the two sites.

Why not add a link to the Kerboodle login page within your Frog account? Alternatively, you can create links in Kerboodle for pasting into your VLE that will give access to specific parts of a course. Enter a Kerboodle course, and use the Copy Link to VLE function under the More button in Lessons, Resources, or Assessment.

Students and teachers will still need to login to Kerboodle, but they will find the right course and content more quickly.

If you used to use the student self-registration function don’t worry - It is very simple to create new user accounts directly in Kerboodle. This process is handled by the school’s single new Kerboodle Administrator (we recommend a data manager, or IT technician, if possible) and is a simple spreadsheet upload. This Getting started guide for the Administrator will take you through the steps. You can find out more about the Administrator on the Administrator help pages.

From September you will be able to give students access to the courses, and the correct groups, by creating group codes, just like in current Kerboodle.

Q: How do I set up my users?

A:

Student users will be created by the school’s single new Kerboodle Administrator, using a simple spreadsheet upload. This Getting started guide for the Administrator will take you through the steps. You can find out more about the Administrator on the Administrator help pages.

From September you will be able to give students access to the courses, and the correct groups, by creating group codes, just like in current Kerboodle.

Q: Will administration be the same?

A:

A lot of the administration will be similar, but there is one key difference. In current Kerboodle multiple teachers in your school can actually be Teachers/Admins. In new Kerboodle only one Administrator account can be held.

Having more than one admin user, who is allowed to create student accounts, led to complications with students having more than one set of login details, or one staff member deleting or altering the accounts created by another.

In new Kerboodle, group management is still easily controlled by individual teachers, but there is only one Administrator in the whole school. Their only task is to create user accounts on behalf of the teachers.

They can easily bulk create student accounts using the upload function. We strongly recommend that you nominate a data manager, IT Technician, or other central, technically-confident person to carry out this role.

Further information and full support is available on the dedicated Administrator support pages of this site.

Q: Why does new Kerboodle only allow one Administrator?

A:

We understand that this is a significant change to your previous Kerboodle experience, but it was made with the day-to-day experience of teachers and students being of primary concern.

Many schools have a number of departments that use Kerboodle courses, and a student may be accessing Kerboodle throughout their school years. With more than one Administrator schools frequently experience difficulties with students and teachers having more than one login for their different courses, or with one staff member accidentally making changes to other department’s users.

Equally, a number of teachers found themselves manually creating all of their student accounts as they weren’t sure how to manage a bulk account creation.

New Kerboodle has been designed to only require one Administrator - who needs to do a minimal amount of work. Course access and Group creation can both be handled by Teachers.

We strongly recommend that your Administrator is very comfortable with using your School Management System (e.g. SIMs) - ideally they will be a Data Manager or IT Technician. That way they should find the simple CSV upload to Kerboodle very straightforward, and will easily and quickly create unique accounts for the students and teachers that need it. They can therefore help to ensure that all users in your school have a streamlined experience across all of their courses.

Q: How do I set up groups?

A:

From September you will set up groups by creating an access code - just like in Kerboodle currently. Create a code within a course, and give it to the students in your group.

You can also create other types of group for your own assignment and reporting purposes, that the students will not have any knowledge of.

Q: How do I give my students access to a course?

A:

You can create and use a group code, just like in Kerboodle currently. Or you can simply add a whole year group from the user management.

How to use new Kerboodle

Q: How do I add my own resources to a course?

A:

It’s easy! Click on Upload from most modules, choose where in the course you want to save the item, then browse for your file. For videos and audio we recommend that you use the Create button, and add a weblink to another site or your VLE, instead of uploading your files. This is so you can access these bigger items quickly and easily when running your lessons. You will not be able to upload video and audio directly into Kerboodle.

Q: How do I create lessons?

A:

The Lessons function has been updated in new Kerboodle! As well as linking together separate resources, you can now prepare online presentation screens, and a notes page for teachers, to accompany all of the resources. You also don’t need to assign your lessons anymore; once created, they are freely available for students and staff to access. But don’t worry, they can’t edit your version of the lesson.

Q: Can I create lessons now for September?

A:

Courses originally published by Nelson Thornes will be available in new Kerboodle from September only, so it will not be possible to create lessons before then.

Q: How do I create assignments?

A:

In most courses on new Kerboodle there will be a special Assessment tab. Here, you simply select an item, and choose Assign from the options. You add a deadline, and choose who to send it to. That’s it! A complete record of all assignments made this way will be available to you from the dedicated Markbook tab.

Content held in the Assessment module can only be seen by students if you assign it to them. But students have open access to a large number of other resources contained in other modules in new Kerboodle courses, such as the Resources module. But you can easily change what they can see. Simply select an item, open the preview pane, and change the setting, to either hide or reveal content to students.

Q: Can I create assignments now for September?

A:

Courses originally published by Nelson Thornes will be available in new Kerboodle from September only, so it will not be possible to create assignments before then.

You can continue to use current Kerboodle until the end of the Summer term for assignments. But you must export and save any marks data that you wish to keep, as it will not be available to you from new Kerboodle. Any assignments for use after September will need to be created in the new Kerboodle once your course is available.

Q: How do I track my students' progress?

A: All assignments you make in new Kerboodle are logged and recorded in the dedicated Markbook. From here, you can view each individual assignment, including all the marks, and all of the completed work.

You can also run one of three reports from the Markbook: Group, Group Diagnostic, and Student. All three reports can be run across multiple tests at the same time, and the Group reports display students’ individual results, as well as a group average.

All reports can be printed to create a PDF for saving, or a hard copy. They can also be exported to create a CSV (like a spreadsheet) ready for importing directly into another school system.

Q: Will digital books work in the same way?

A: Digital books in new Kerboodle have most of the same features. One or two of the less used features have been removed, with the addition of a personal notebook for teaching notes or students’ revision; a spotlight tool; and a Resources button for quick access to course content. Visit the Digital Book help pages of this support site for full information.
