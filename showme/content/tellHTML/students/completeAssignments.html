What are Assignments?

Your teachers might use Kerboodle to set assignments for you. They might assign you a lesson you have worked through in class, or set an assignment with a homework task or revision activities.

The good thing about assignments is that all your resources can be accessed in one window through a menu, so you don't need to search the library for all the activities.

Once assigned, they will appear on your course page or Today's Events panel so you can keep track of your workload.

You can send work back through assignments for your teacher to mark. Your teacher will use these marks to track your progress.

Accessing your Assignments

Any current assignments will appear in the Today's Events panel, or they can also be accessed through the course page.

Just click on the assignment title and it will open in a new window. Use the Menu bar to move between the resources within the assignment.

How? For step-by-step instructions, please have a look at our Show Me How tutorial: Show Me How!

Return work as part of an Assignment

When you complete your assignment, any Test Yourself marks will automatically be returned to your teacher, but you may also have the option to send a file back to them for marking (for example, a worksheet).

If you have been asked to complete a worksheet that has been assigned to you, you will need to save a copy first before completing the worksheet. Alternatively, your teacher might ask you to create your own file to submit.

Once you have completed your work, use the Menu bar to go back to the Introduction page and use the Browse button* to find the file to submit back to your teacher.

*If the Browse button is greyed out, your teacher has not requested that you return a document.
