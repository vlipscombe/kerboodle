Also Practice

This section is your library of content, where you can browse, preview and play.

Work completed here is not saved or recorded.

To open an item click once on the title. To find out more information about it without having to open it, select the tickbox to the left of the title, then choose Preview from above the list.

To find an item use the folder structure on the left-hand side. Clicking on a title in here will filter the main list of content to just the items within your chosen section. Expand and collapse this menu using the plus and minus buttons.

To add your own content, use the Upload and Create buttons above the folder structure, to the top left of your screen.

To preview, copy, edit, move or delete, first select an item from the list (using the checkbox to the left of the item title) then use the buttons above the list. You can preview and copy any item. Other functions will be unavailable unless the item belongs to you, so you may need to create a copy first. Some of these functions will only be available if a single item is selected.

To learn about the student experience visit the Student support pages.

If you cannot resolve your query, please Contact Us.

Frequently asked questions

Search and Open

Q: How do I find an item?

A: Use the folder structure on the left-hand side of the screen. Clicking on one of these folders will filter the number of items shown in the main list to help you find the one you are looking for. Expand and collapse the folder menu by clicking on the text and/or the plus and minus signs.

Q: How do I open an item?

A: Click once on the title to open an item. Some items will open in your internet browser, others (such as Microsoft Office documents) might need to download first.

Q: How does the Search work?

A: Enter a keyword into the Search bar, and/or choose an option from the ‘Advanced’ menu just to the right of the Search button. Click Search. The results of your search will appear in the main list. To clear your search, click on the small cross next to the search terms.

If your search does not find sufficient items, try reducing the number of words used.

Q: Where can I find more information on an item?

A: Select any one item (using the tickbox to the left of the title) and click on Preview above the list. This will open a preview window to the right of your screen, which will contain a thumbnail image, and lots more information. You can change the item you are previewing by clicking somewhere in another row (but clicking on the title in a row will still open the item.) If you have selected more than one item then the Preview function will not be available to you.

Q: How do I bookmark an item?

A: Click on the small star to the left of the item title – the star will turn yellow, marking it as a Favourite. To retrieve your Favourites, click on the My Favourites filter on the left-hand side of your screen.

Q: How do I find only my content?

A: Click on the ‘My Resources’ filter to the top left of the screen. This will filter the main list to only your items, as identified in the Source column.

Q: How do I find only the original course content?

A: Above the list is a tickbox labelled Show Oxford content only. Selecting this will mean that any searches and filters you use will only produce original content – content identified as Oxford in the Source column.

Q: Can I use this with my VLE?

A: You can create links in your online product to save in your VLE. These links will point to a specific location, not to items of content.

When working in your VLE, you can then click on this link to open the course. If you are not already logged into the site then you will be prompted to login to gain access.

To create a link login to http://www.kerboodle.com. Go to the course, section (Resources, Lessons, Homework, Practice, or Assessment) and folder (using the left-hand folder structure) that you would like to create a link to. Then click on the More button above the main list. Choose Copy Link to VLE. Click on the link to select it, then use Ctrl + C, or Cmd + C to copy. You can then paste this link into your VLE in the usual way.

Students will be able to use these links from your VLE as well, but a link to the Assessment or Homework section will not work for them - as content here has to be assigned first.

Q: Can I print a contents list?

A:

Yes you can. To print a list of all of the content, click on the ‘All’ filter to the top left of the screen. Then choose Print search results from the More menu above the list of content. A list of the items currently displayed in the main area of the page will display. Click Print to print a paper copy of this list.

To print a list of selected items of content, use the folder structure on the left hand-side of your screen. Clicking on a title in here will filter the main list of content to just the items within your chosen section. Then choose Print search results from the More menu above the list of content. A list of the items currently displayed in the main area of the page will display. Click Print to print a paper copy of this list.

Upload and Create

Q: How do I add my own content?

A: Click on Upload at the top left of your screen to add content to this part of the course. Click on Browse to find the file on your computer or network. If you wish, you can select a folder in which to place your item. If no folder is selected, your item will be uploaded to your current place in the folder structure. Enter a title, and click Upload.

If you later wish to move your item of content, select the item using the tickbox, and choose Move from the More menu above the list of content.

You can quickly find your own content by clicking on the My Resources filter, just below the Upload button.

To change the title, other information, or student access, select the item using the tickbox, and click Preview above the list of content. This will open the Preview pane, within which you can change much of the item’s information. Click in a box to change the text. Remember to click Save Changes at the bottom of the Preview pane when you are finished.

You cannot add items that are over 5MB in size, and some file types may not be accepted. Video and audio files are among those that cannot be uploaded.

Always keep a back-up of your own files.

Q: Can I add new folders?

A: Yes. Click on Create and choose New/Edit folder. Choose where you would like to add a folder, by clicking on the desired location on the left-hand side. Then click ‘Add folder’. Your new folder will appear on the left. You can rename this by changing the text in the Folder name field, and clicking update. You can also delete any of your folders from this window.

Q: Can I create a link to a website?

A: Yes. Click on Create and choose Web Link. Enter a website URL prefixed with http:// i.e. http://oup.com. You will be required to add a title, but all other metadata such as weblink location, description and keywords are optional. The weblink will open in a new browser tab/window, depending on browser settings.

To edit any of this information, or student access, select the weblink using the tickbox, and click Preview above the list of content. This will open the Preview pane, within which you can change much of the item’s information. Click in a box to change the text. Remember to click Save Changes at the bottom of the Preview pane when you are finished

Copy, Move, Edit and Delete

Q: How do I copy an item?

A: Select an item using the tickbox to the left of the title. Click on Copy, above the list of content. If you wish to change the title of the new copy you may do so by clicking in the title field. Choose where you would like the copy to be saved from the folder structure. Click Save when you are finished. The Source column will identify the item as your object, which allows you to move, edit (depending on the object type) and delete the item.

If you selected more than one object the Copy function will not be available to you.

Q: How do I move an item?

A: Select the item(s) you wish to move using the tickbox to the left of the title. Then click More above the list of items, and choose Move. Select the destination for your item(s) by clicking on the folder title. Then click Save Changes.

Note that you can only move items which are yours, as indicated in the Source column of the item list. If you wish to move an item that is not yours, you must first copy it.

Q: How do I delete an item?

A: Select the item(s) you wish to delete, and click More (above the list of items) then choose Delete. You will be asked to confirm the deletion – click OK if you wish to go ahead.

Note that you can only delete your own items, that is, items where you are identified in the Source column.

Q: Can I make changes to/edit an item?

A: You can edit the information (such as title and description) about any object that is yours. To do this, select a single object, and click on the Preview button above the list of content. This will open the Preview pane, within which you can change much of the item’s information. Click in a box to change the text. Remember to click Save Changes at the bottom of the Preview pane when you are finished.

Items such as Microsoft Office files can also be edited. Open the object and save a version locally – on your desktop, or your network. You can then edit this file. When you are finished, use the Upload button at the top left of your screen to add the edited item to your course. This will not replace the original copy.

Items that you cannot edit include: video, audio, animations, and interactive activities.

Q: Can I change the title of an item, or other information fields?

A: Yes. You can edit the information (such as title and description) about any object that is yours. To do this, select a single object, and click on the Preview button above the list of content. This will open the Preview pane, within which you can change much of the item’s information. Click in a box to change the text. Remember to click Save Changes at the bottom of the Preview pane when you are finished.

Note that you can only change the information of items which are yours, as indicated in the Source column of the item list. If you wish to alter an item that is not yours, you must first copy it.

Student access

Q: How do I find out and change what students can access?

A: There is a column titled Visible to students. Students can only see and access items that have a tick in this column. To change student access to an individual item, select the item using the tickbox to the left of the item’s title. Then click Preview above the list. In the open preview pane, scroll down to find the Visible to Students field. Tick or untick this accordingly. Scroll to the bottom of the pane and click Save Changes.

Q: How do I assign an object to my students?

A: Students with permission to access this course can freely find any object that has a tick in the Visible to Students column. You can change this status - select the item using the tickbox, and click on Preview above the list of content. In the open preview pane, scroll down to find the Visible to Students field. Tick or untick this accordingly. Scroll to the bottom of the pane and click Save Changes.

Objects that can be assigned, and tracked through the Markbook, can be found in the Assessment or Homework module. Visit the Assessment/Homework support pages for more information.

Understanding the screen

Q: Why are some things greyed-out and inactive?

A:

  • If a button that produces an action is greyed out (such as Edit, or Preview) you either need to select an item first (using the tickbox to the left of the title) or you might have made an inappropriate selection. Note that the majority of functions can only be carried out on one item at a time; selecting multiple items will cause the button to become inactive.
  • If a section of the left-hand folder structure is greyed out, it is because your institution has not purchased this part of the product. Clicking on the greyed-out text will give you the option to visit the Oxford website to find out more information.
  • If one of the tabs within a course, to the top right of your screen, is greyed-out, this is because your institution has not purchased this part of the product. If you think you should have access, contact your system Administrator. If you want to find out more about purchasing options, click on the tab itself for a link to the Oxford website.

Q: What are the icons in the list of content?

A: To the left of the title are 3 objects.

  • The first is an icon that indicates the format. To see what they stand for, click on Advanced, to the right of the Search bar at the top of your screen.
  • The middle is the outline of a star – click on this to bookmark your favourite items. Recall this list by clicking on My Favourites to the top left of your screen.
  • The furthest left is a tickbox – use this to select an item before carrying out an action such as Edit or Move.

Q: What are the Page Range boxes?

A: At the top right of your screen there may be two boxes with Page Range written underneath. These are for courses that have a book associated with them, and they will reflect the part of the course you are working in, according to the left-hand folder structure. When you have clicked on a folder on the left-hand side that is associated with a book, the book title, and page range, will be displayed in this area.

If you have access to the online version of this book (in a Digital Book section of your online product) then clicking on one of the page icons will open the Digital Book in a new tab or window, at the correct page.
