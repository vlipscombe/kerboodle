Markbook

At a glance

This section is the record of all work you have sent to your students, as well as the place where you see submitted work, give marks, and run reports.

Your students will not see any content, unless you release it to them by making an assignment.

To find the work of a particular Group, select the Group from the menu on the left.

To find the work of a particular Student, first select their group on the left, then click on the Students tab at the top. Then click on the student’s name. Their work will display in the main list.

Open an item by clicking on the title. This will show you the original work set, as well as the students’ submissions. You allocate a mark to student work from this window tool.

To learn about the student experience visit the Student support pages.

If you cannot resolve your query, please Contact us.

Frequently asked questions

Search and Open

Q: How do I find an item?

A: You can use the filters to the top left of your screen. These will allow you to filter the screen and show only assignments of a particular status, for example, only those that are past their due date but still missing some student work.

You can also search by Group, or Student. Select the Group on the left-hand side that you wish to view. Only that Group’s assignments will now be visible in the main list. To view an individual student’s assignments, first select their group. Then click on the Students tab towards the top left of your screen. All of the students in the selected group will now be displayed. Click on one student name to display only their work in the main screen.

The keyword search is also available – this will search the assignment titles only.

Viewing Work, Viewing Marks and Giving Marks

Q: How do I see how my class is doing?

A: Find your Group using the left-hand menu – click on the Group name, and all of that Group’s assignments will appear in the main screen.

Q: How do I view a student’s marks?

A: Find the student’s group in the left-hand menu. With the Group selected, click on the Students tab towards the top left of your screen. All of the students in the selected group will now be displayed. Click on one student name to display only their work in the main screen.

Q: How do I view a piece of student work?

A: First you need to find the assignment in question – see How do I find an assignment? above for help with this. Click on the title to open it.

The details about this assignment, including the original piece of work, will be displayed in the top left of the window. All of the students it was assigned to will be displayed top right. Make sure the student in question is highlighted in green, and that their name is displayed in large below.

The work that they submitted will be accessible in the bottom section of this window. Click on the work to open and view it.

If you find that you cannot click on the name of the student in question, in the top right of this window, it means that they have not submitted their work.

Note that the display will look different if you located the assignment by first choosing an individual student, then clicking on the title of the assignment.

Q: How do I find any marking that I need to do?

A: In the Markbook you will see a filter towards the top left, called ‘For manual marking’. Clicking on this filter will show only assignments that contain marking that you need to do.

There is a shortcut to this filter from your Course Homepage (to the top right).

Q: How do I give a mark to a student?

A: Find the assignment that you wish to mark (see How do I find an assignment? above for help with this), and click on the title to open it.

The details about this assignment, including the original piece of work, will be displayed in the top left of the window. All of the students it was assigned to will be displayed top right. Make sure the student in question is highlighted in green, and that their name is displayed in large below.

The work that they submitted will be accessible in the bottom section of this window. (Click on the work to view it.) You enter their mark in the box to the bottom right of the window. When you have entered the mark, click Submit Marks, then Save and Close.

If you find that you cannot click on the name of the student in question, in the top right of this window, it means that they have not submitted their work.

Note that the display will be arranged differently if you located the assignment by first choosing an individual student, then clicking on the title of the assignment.

Q: How do I find the marks from auto-marked assignments?

A: Find the assignment (see How do I find an assignment? above for help with this) and click on the title. All of the students it was assigned to will be displayed top right. Clicking on each student in turn will show you their marks below, if they have completed the assignment. If they have not completed the work, you will not be able to click on their name.

Note that the display will look different if you located the assignment by first choosing an individual student, then clicking on the title of the assignment.

Q: Can I add a score to the Markbook if the work is given in by hand?

A: Yes. This option is only possible if the student has been assigned a non-interactive piece of work, usually a Word document or a PDF. When the student accesses the assignment, there is a tickbox they can select to say that they will give the work in by hand. They then need to click ‘Submit’. If the box has been ticked, it allows you to give a mark for the assignment, even though the student has given in their work to you in person.

Q: A student needs to edit an assignment that has already been submitted - what do I do?

A: If a student has submitted an assignment but wants to make changes to it, such as changing the file or unticking the ‘I am giving my work in by hand’ box, you will need to make a brand new assignment. If you would like to withdraw the original assignment, please note that this will permanently remove the assignment for all students.

Using the Markbook

Q: How do I add a new row to the Markbook?

A: You must make an assignment from the Assessment (or Homework) module. This automatically generates a row into the Markbook, for tracking and marking that piece of work. See How do I send an item to my students? in the Assessment/Homework module pages for more information.

Q: How do I see which assignments are due soon, or incomplete?

A:

There are a number of filters to the top left of the Markbook screen which will show you assignments according to their status:

  • Current assignments will show you any assignment that has been assigned to students which hasn’t passed its end date
  • Due today will show you assignments whose due date is today
  • Due this week will show you assignments whose due date falls in this calendar (Monday to Sunday) week
  • Expired (incomplete submission) will show you assignments that are now past their due date, but are missing some student submissions. (Note that students cannot submit work after the due date. You can choose to give an extension to the assignment. Simply change the due date. This will apply to all students who have been assigned this piece of work.)
  • For manual marking will show you any assignment that contains marking that you need to do
  • Fully submitted and marked will show you all assignments that are totally complete.

Q: Can I make changes to an assignment after I have sent it to my students?

A: You can change the due date, even after the current due date has passed. This is a good way of giving an extension, and the changes apply to every student that was sent this particular piece of work. To do this, open the assignment by clicking on the title in the Markbook, then change the due date by clicking on the end date and choosing a new date from the calendar. Click on Save, then click Save and Close, bottom right.

You can also choose to Withdraw the assignment. Open the assignment by clicking on the title in the Markbook, then click on Withdraw Assignment, in the bottom left of the window. If any student has already completed the work, the record of their work will be lost when you withdraw the assignment.

If you wish to make any changes to an assignment other than the due date, you will need to withdraw the current assignment, then re-assign it from the Assessment (or Homework) module.

Q: Can I cancel an assignment?

A: Yes. Open the assignment by clicking on the title in the Markbook, then click on Withdraw Assignment, in the bottom left of the window. Please note that withdrawing will permanently remove the assignment for all students, so if any student has already completed the work, the record of their work will be lost.

Q: Can I run reports?

A: Yes you can. There are three reports which can be run from your Markbook: Student, Group and Diagnostic. Each report can be printed as a PDF document or exported as a CSV file.

The Student Report allows you to track the performance of individual students across all of their assignments in that course.

The Group Report allows you to view the average score within the group for each assignment. You can expand the assignment to view the marks for each individual students, allowing you to compare performance across the group.

The Diagnostic Report gives you more detail on question by question achievement in interactive assignments. The report helps to interpret which areas of a topic the group is achieving well in, and which they are struggling more with.

For more information on how to access and use the reports, see the FAQs below.

Q: How do I access and use the Student Report?

A: To access the Student Report, click on a group to the left hand side of your screen. Next, click on the Students tab and select a student from the list. Then, click the Student Report button above the assignment titles.

In the pop-up that displays, you will see a list of all of the assignments the student has been assigned. If a student has ‘Incomplete Submission’ next to their name, it means that they have not yet handed in their assignment. If a student has ‘Incomplete Marking’ next to their name, it means that you need to mark their assignment. You will need to close the report and return to the main Markbook screen in order to mark the assignment. See How do I give a mark to a student? if you need help with this.

If a student has submitted their assignment, the mark will be displayed to the right of the title. Clicking on the assignment title will either open or download the student’s submitted work, depending on whether the submission was an interactive test or a document.

To print the report as a PDF document, click the Print button at the bottom of the pop up. To export the report as a CSV file, click Export.

Q: How do I access and use the Group Report?

A: To access the Group Report, click on a group to the left hand side of your screen. Next, select the assignments you wish to run the report on by checking the tickbox next to each assignment title. Then, click the Group Report button above the assignment titles.

In the pop-up that displays, you will see a list of all of the assignments you selected. Each assignment shows the average score for that group. Clicking on the title of each assignment displays a drop down listing all of the students who were set this work. If a student’s name is highlighted in green, this means they have submitted the assignment and their score will display in the Score column. Clicking on the name of a student who has submitted either opens or downloads their submitted work, depending on whether the submission was an interactive test or a document.

If a student has ‘Incomplete Submission’ next to their name, it means that they have not yet handed in their assignment. If a student has ‘Incomplete Marking’ next to their name, it means that you need to mark their assignment. You will need to close the report and return to the main Markbook screen in order to mark the assignment. See How do I give a mark to a student? if you need help with this. Please note that the average group score is only calculated from students whose assignment has been both submitted and marked.

To print the report as a PDF document, click the Print button at the bottom of the pop up. To export the report as a CSV file, click Export.

Q: How do I access and use the Diagnostic Report?

A: To access the Diagnostic Report, click on a group to the left hand side of your screen. Next, select the assignments you wish to run the report on by checking the tickbox next to each assignment title. Then, click the Diagnostic Report button above the assignment titles.

In the pop-up that displays, you will see a list of all of the assignments you selected. Each assignment shows the average score for that group. Clicking on the title of each assignment displays a drop down listing all of the students who were set this work. If a student’s name is highlighted in green, this means they have submitted the assignment and their score will display in the Score column. Clicking on the name of a student who has submitted either opens or downloads their submitted work, depending on whether the submission was an interactive test or a document.

The diagnostic report is for use with interactive tests, identifying exactly which questions students have been having problems with. The student’s mark per question will be set on a red background if they scored 0% for that question, on an orange background if they scored between 1-99% and on a green background if they scored 100%. This allows quick analysis of your class on a question by question basis. Clicking on the score for a question opens that screen of the student’s submitted interactive test.

For information about the circular icons, see What do the self-selected confidence icons represent? below.

If a student has ‘Incomplete Submission’ next to their name, it means that they have not yet handed in their assignment. If a student has ‘Incomplete Marking’ next to their name, it means that you need to mark their assignment. You will need to close the report and return to the main Markbook screen in order to mark the assignment. See How do I give a mark to a student? if you need help with this. Please note that the average group score is only calculated from students whose assignment has been both submitted and marked.

To print the report as a PDF document, click the Print button at the bottom of the pop-up. To export the report as a CSV file, click Export.

Q: What do the coloured boxes in the Diagnostic Report mean?

A: If the Diagnostic Report is run on an interactive assignment, you are able to see a visual representation of each student’s attainment per question. If a student has scored 0% for that question, the box containing the score will be red. If a student has scored 1-99% for that question, the box will be orange. If a student has scored 100% for that question, the box will be green. This allows quick analysis of your class on a question by question basis. Clicking on the score for a question opens that screen of the student’s submitted interactive test.

Q: What do the self-selected confidence icons represent?

A: Some courses have formative assignments which prompt the student to indicate how confident they feel about the topic. The selected option is visible to Teachers in the diagnostic markbook report, immediately to the right of the question by question breakdown per student. The red icon indicates a low level of confidence regarding that assignment, the orange icon indicates an average level of confidence, and the green icon indicates a high level of confidence.

Q: Can I export/download the marks to use elsewhere?

A: Yes you can. When the report is open there are two options at the bottom of the pop up: Print and Export. The print option either opens or downloads (depending on your browser settings) a PDF file which can then be saved and printed. The Export option either opens or downloads (depending on your browser settings) a CSV file which can then be used elsewhere.

Understanding the screen

Q: Why are some things greyed-out and inactive?

A:

  • If a button that produces an action is greyed out (such as Preview) you either need to select an item first (using the tickbox to the left of the title) or you might have made an inappropriate selection. Note that the majority of functions can only be carried out on one item at a time; selecting multiple items will cause the button to become inactive.
  • If one of the tabs within a course, to the top right of your screen, is greyed-out, this is because your institution has not purchased this part of the product. If you think you should have access, contact your system Administrator. If you want to find out more about purchasing options, click on the tab itself for a link to the Oxford website.
  • If the Students tab is greyed out, it is because you must first select a group. Return to the Groups tab, select a group, then click on the Students tab.

Q: What are the icons in the list of content?

A: To the left of the title are 2 objects.

  • The one on the right is an icon that tells you the status of the assignment. They will also be found next to the filters, towards the top left of your screen.
  • The one on the left is a tickbox – use this to select an item before carrying out an action such as Preview.
