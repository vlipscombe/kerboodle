User management

At a glance

This section is where you can organise your teaching groups, and reset student passwords.

If your institution is completely new to the product, you will be asked to invite a system Administrator. If your institution already has an Administrator, their name will be displayed in your profile (click on your name at the top of the screen).

To add users contact your System Administrator.

To learn about the student experience visit the Student support pages.

If you cannot resolve your query, please Contact us.

Frequently asked questions

The Administrator role

Q: What is the Administrator for?

A: Multiple departments using different courses can all purchase access to products at http://www.kerboodle.com. Having one administrative user ensures that each student and teacher only has one set of login details to get to all of their courses.

The Administrator does not have access to the content of courses - their only responsibility is creating user accounts and Global Groups (groups that exist across the whole institution).

Your Administrator should be someone who is familiar with your School Management System, so that they can easily export student data and create student logins in Kerboodle.

Visit the Administrator support pages to see all of the information available.

Q: Who is my Administrator?

A: If your institution does not already have an Administrator in Kerboodle, you will be prompted to invite one when you visit the User Management module.

If you do not receive this prompt, or you can see that there are already some students in the User Management module, then your institution already has an Administrator. To find out who they are, click on your name at the very top of the screen. Scroll to the bottom of the My Profile window that opens, and the Administrator’s details will be shown at the bottom.

Q: The Administrator has left without sharing their login - what should we do?

A: If you are sure that the Administrator is no longer at your institution, and you do not have the login details, then Contact us and we will be able to help.

Adding students and teachers

Q: How do I add colleagues?

A: If your system does not yet have an Administrator, you can invite your colleagues to join, using the Invite Teachers button. This will send them an email, asking them to activate their account. Note that if they do not receive this email, they should check their Junk/Spam folder.

If your institution does have an Administrator, only they can add further users. Find out who the Administrator is by clicking on your name at the very top of the screen. The Administrator’s details will be displayed at the bottom of the window that opens.

Q: How do I add students?

A: Only the Administrator can add students - this ensures that students only receive one set of login details if your online product is used by multiple departments.

If your institution already has an Administrator, you can discover who they are by clicking on your name at the very top of the screen. Their details will be shown at the bottom of the My Profile window that opens.

If there is not already an Administrator in place you will be asked to invite one each time you enter the User Management module. Entering their details generates emails asking them to activate their account, and explaining all about the system and how to add students.

Once students have been added to the system, it is easy to give them access to the course. See ‘Why can’t I see my students or groups?’ below.

Q: How do I give students access to a course?

A: To add students to a course, click on the User Management tab. Under the Manage Course Groups button to the top left of your screen, select the Add global groups to course option. Tick the groups that need to have access to the course, and click Save. You can check what students you have added to the course by clicking on the All Students filter.

Please note that the User Management system is shared amongst all Teachers within a course. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course, as will any changes any other Teacher may make.

Q: Why can’t I see my students or groups?

A: It’s possible that your institution does not yet have an Administrator. Administrators are responsible for creating user accounts and global groups. To see if this is the case, click on your name at the top of the screen and scroll down to the bottom. If you do not have an Administrator, there will be no details listed. To invite an Administrator to your institution, click on the User Management tab and follow the instructions.

If your institution does have an Administrator but you still can’t see your students or groups, this means that the students have not yet been added to that course. To add students to a course, click on the User Management tab. Under the Manage Course Groups button to the top left of your screen, select the Add global groups to course option. Tick the groups that need to have access to the course, and click Save. You can check what students you have added to the course by clicking on the All Students filter.

Q: Why can I no longer see the students I added?

A: If students you have added to a course through the User Management tab have disappeared, it’s likely that another Teacher with access to that course has removed them. The User Management system is shared amongst all Teachers within a course. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course, as will any changes any other Teacher may make.

To re-add your students to a course, see How do I give students access to a course? above.

Q: Will the users be pre-loaded for me?

A: No, users will have to be loaded in by your Administrator.

If your institution does not already have an Administrator in Kerboodle, you will be prompted to invite one when you visit the User Management module.

If you do not receive this prompt, or you can see that there are already some students in the User Management module, then your institution already has an Administrator. To find out who they are, click on your name at the very top of the screen. Scroll to the bottom of the My Profile window that opens, and the Administrator’s details will be shown at the bottom.

Q: Is there a service to load students for me?

A: There is currently no service like this provided.

Q: Can users be archived?

A: Yes, both Teacher and Student users can be archived. Archived users can no longer log in to the platform, but all of their annotations, content (teachers only) and marks (students only) can still be accessed. Only Administrators can archive users. For more information on archiving and on the Administrator role visit our Administrator support pages .

Creating and editing Groups

Q: How do I create a Group?

A: To create a Group, the student users must already exist in the system. If they do not, you need to contact your Administrator.

There are two levels of groups: Global groups, and Course Groups.

Global groups are created by the Administrator, as part of the bulk upload process, and exist regardless of which course you are in. Only your Administrator can edit or delete these groups. But as a Teacher user you can add or remove these Groups from your course.

Within a course, you can place existing student users in as many groups as you like. Note that these groups will only exist within the course that you are in. Click on Manage Course Groups to the top left of your screen, and choose Create/Edit Course Group.

Select the Global Group on the left in which you wish to create a Course Group. Then choose Create New Group on the right. Give the Group a name, and select the students from the list by clicking on the rows. Click Save and then Save Changes.

All teachers using this course will be able to see, but not edit, your Course Groups.

Q: How do I edit a Group?

A: Any Teacher within a course can edit any Course Group. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course.

To edit Course Groups, click on Manage Course Groups to the top left of your screen. Choose Create/Edit Course Groups. Select the Group you wish to edit on the left hand side, then choose Edit Group, on the right. Change the name, or add and remove students. Then click Save, followed by Save Changes.

Q: How do I delete a Group?

A: Any Teacher within a course can edit any Course Group. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course.

To edit Course Groups, click on Manage Course Groups to the top left of your screen. Choose Create/Edit Course Groups. Select the Group you wish to delete on the left hand side, then choose Delete Group, on the right. Confirm your action by clicking OK, or click Cancel if you wish to stop the deletion. Then click Save Changes.

Q: Can other teachers edit groups I’ve created?

A: Any Teacher within a course can edit and delete any Course Group. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course, as will any changes any other Teacher may make.

Logging in, usernames and passwords

Q: What information is needed to log in?

A:

To login, each user needs a username, password, and the institution code.

Q: I can’t log in - what should I do?

A: Visit our dedicated Logging In help page for support.

Q: My students can’t log in - what can I do?

A: Visit our dedicated Logging In help page for support.

Q: I’ve forgotten my password - what should I do?

A: Visit our dedicated Logging In help page for support.

Q: How are the student logins created?

A: Your system Administrator will create the student logins in bulk, by exporting their names and groups from your institution’s management system.

The default usernames are the first letter of the first name, followed by the surname. For example, Sally Smith will have a username of: ssmith

If two students’ names will create identical usernames, then one of them will have a number automatically added on to the end. Look at their entries in the User Management module to identify these users.

For first login, the student’s password will be the same as their username. The system will force them to change this password when they first log in. It must be 6 characters or more.

The students will also need their institution code. This is the same as yours, and is provided to you at the same time as your login details, or by your system Administrator.

Q: My student has forgotten their password - what can I do?

A:

You can reset their password - this will return it to the default, which is the same as their username. Find the student in the User Management module, using the Search bar, or the Groups filters on the left. Select the student from the list, and click on Preview from above the list. This will open the Preview pane. In it there is an option Reset Password. Click on the tickbox, then click Save Changes at the bottom. The student’s password is now reset.
