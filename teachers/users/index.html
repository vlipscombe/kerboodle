User management

At a glance

This section is where you can organise your teaching groups, and reset student passwords.

If your institution is completely new to the product, you will be asked to invite a system Administrator. Please see our teachers getting started guide which will help you with the intial setup of Kerboodle.

If your institution already has an Administrator, their name will be displayed in your profile (click on your name at the top of the screen).

To add users contact your System Administrator. As a teacher you will still be able to create your own course and assignment groups once your students have been imported into Kerboodle by your Administrator.

To learn about the student experience visit the Student support pages.

If you cannot resolve your query, please Contact us.

Update to the student import process

In response to user feedback, we have recently simplified the student import process. The Class column has now been removed from the CSV template file, and the Form column is now optional.

Please use the updated CSV template file when importing users. You can download this by clicking on the CSV dropdown menu in the centre console and selecting Download CSV template. Please do not remove, reorder or change the column headings.

Frequently asked questions

The Administrator role

Q: What is the Administrator for?

A: Multiple departments using different courses can all purchase access to products at http://www.kerboodle.com. Having one administrative user ensures that each student and teacher only has one set of login details to get to all of their courses.

The Administrator does not have access to the content of courses - their only responsibility is creating user accounts and Global Groups (groups that exist across the whole institution).

Your Administrator should be someone who is familiar with your School Management System, so that they can easily export student data and create student logins in Kerboodle.

Visit the Administrator support pages to see all of the information available.

Q: Who is my Administrator?

A: If your institution does not already have an Administrator in Kerboodle, you will be prompted to invite one when you visit the User Management module.

If you do not receive this prompt, or you can see that there are already some students in the User Management module, then your institution already has an Administrator. To find out who they are, click on your name at the very top of the screen. Scroll to the bottom of the My Profile window that opens, and the Administrator’s details will be shown at the bottom.

Q: The Administrator has left without sharing their login - what should we do?

A: If you are sure that the Administrator is no longer at your institution, and you do not have the login details, then Contact us and we will be able to help.

Adding students and teachers

Q: How do I add colleagues?

A: If your system does not yet have an Administrator, you can invite your colleagues to join, using the Invite Teachers button. This will send them an email, asking them to activate their account. Note that if they do not receive this email, they should check their Junk/Spam folder.

If your institution does have an Administrator, only they can add further users. Find out who the Administrator is by clicking on your name at the very top of the screen. The Administrator’s details will be displayed at the bottom of the window that opens.

Q: How do I add students?

A: Only the Administrator can add students - this ensures that students only receive one set of login details if your online product is used by multiple departments.

If your institution already has an Administrator, you can discover who they are by clicking on your name at the very top of the screen. Their details will be shown at the bottom of the My Profile window that opens.

If there is not already an Administrator in place you will be asked to invite one each time you enter the User Management module. Entering their details generates emails asking them to activate their account, and explaining all about the system and how to add students.

Once students have been added to the system, it is easy to give them access to the course. See ‘Why can’t I see my students or groups?’ below.

Q: How do I give students access to a course?

A: Click on Group Access to the left hand side of the screen. There are two methods you can choose between: Instant Access via Global Group , and Access via Group Code.

We strongly recommend that you choose only one of these options per course.

Instant Access via Global Group

  • Click on the Instant Access via Global Groups heading
  • Choose Add/Remove Global Groups.
  • Tick the groups that you wish to have access to the course, then click Save.
  • In the Group Access window click Save Changes. The students selected will now automatically have access to the course, and will be displayed in the main list in the User Management module.

Access via Group Code

Click on the Access via Group Code heading and then Create a Course Code Group. Enter a group name, and when you press Save, a code will automatically be generated. In the main Group Access window click Save Changes to confirm. Give the generated code to your students, and once they’ve entered this on their homepage they have access to the course. For more information about this, see How do students sign themselves up to a group/course using a group code? below.

Please note that the User Management system is shared amongst all Teachers within a course. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course, as will any changes any other Teacher may make.

Q: How can I remove student access to a course?

A: Access is removed from the Group Access window - click on the Group Access button in User Management.

If you have given students access to a course via Global Groups, you remove access by removing the Global Groups. You can recognise a Global Group by this icon:

  • In the Group Access window, click Instant Access via Global Group
  • Click Add/Remove Global Groups
  • untick the groups you no longer wish to have access, then click Save
  • click Save Changes in the main window for the changes to update in the main list.

If you have given students access to a course via a Group Code, you can choose to remove access per student, or remove the whole group. You can recognise a Code Group by this icon:

  • In the Group Access window, select Access via Group Code
  • Select the group that you wish to remove students from, from the left
  • Click Edit Group and its Students. (Alternatively, choose Delete Group, to remove the whole group and all of the students.)
  • Untick the students which you no longer wish to have access. (Or, to remove access to all students in that group, use the Select all tickbox.)
  • Click Save, and then Save Changes in the main window. You may need to refresh the page for the student list to be updated.

Q: Why can’t I see my students or groups?

A: Make sure your students have access to this course. For instructions on how to do this, see How do I give students access to a course?

To find out which students already have accounts in Kerboodle, click on the User Details button in the User Management module of any course. If you need more student accounts creating, contact your Administrator.

To find out who your Administrator is click on your name at the top of the Kerboodle screen - your Administrator’s details will appear at the bottom of your profile.

If your institution does not have an Administrator, there will be no details listed. To invite an Administrator to your institution, click on the User Management tab and follow the instructions.

Q: How do students sign themselves up to a group/course using a group code?

A: Once a student has logged in, they will see a button to the right hand side of their homepage called Enter Group Code. The student enters the code that they have been given and clicks Join Group. The course will immediately appear on their homepage. The student can then click on the course tile to enter the course.

Your students will need to know their login details. To find out their usernames, use the User Details button in any course User Management. On first login their password will match their username. Their institution code will be the same as yours.

Q: Why can I no longer see the students I added?

A: If students you have added to a course through the User Management tab have disappeared, it’s likely that another Teacher with access to that course has removed them. The User Management system is shared amongst all Teachers within a course. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course, as will any changes any other Teacher may make.

To re-add your students to a course, see How do I give students access to a course? above.

Q: Will the users be pre-loaded for me?

A: No, users will have to be loaded in by your Administrator.

If your institution does not already have an Administrator in Kerboodle, you will be prompted to invite one when you visit the User Management module.

If you do not receive this prompt, or you can see that there are already some students in the User Management module, then your institution already has an Administrator. To find out who they are, click on your name at the very top of the screen. Scroll to the bottom of the My Profile window that opens, and the Administrator’s details will be shown at the bottom.

Q: Is there a service to load students for me?

A: There is currently no service like this provided.

Q: Can users be archived?

A: Yes, both Teacher and Student users can be archived. Archived users can no longer log in to the platform, but all of their annotations, content (teachers only) and marks (students only) can still be accessed. Only Administrators can archive users. For more information on archiving and on the Administrator role visit our Administrator support pages .

Creating and editing Groups

Q: What are Group Codes?

A: Group Codes refer to a special type of group which allow students to sign themselves up to a group (and gaining access to the course that the group belongs to) by entering a code on their homepage. The code is automated, but can be changed to something more appropriate if you wish.

For more information on creating Group Codes , see How do I give students access to a course? above.

Q: What are Assignment Groups?

A: Within a course, you can create smaller groups for the purposes of assignments and reporting.. These are called Assignment Groups, and existing student users can be placed in as many of these groups as you like

To create an Assignment Group, see How do I give students access to a course? above.

Q: How do I create a Group?

A: There are three types of group: Global Groups, Code Groups and Assignment Groups.

Global Groups are created by the Administrator, and exist regardless of which course you are in. Only your Administrator can edit or delete these groups. But as a Teacher user you can add or remove these Groups from your course.

Code Groups are created by Teachers and allow students to sign themselves up to a course by entering a code on their homepage. To create a Code Group:

  • click on Group Access to the top left of your screen
  • click the Access via Group Code heading and then the Create a Course Code Group button
  • enter a group name, and when you press Save, a code will automatically be generated
  • in the Group Access window click Save Changes to confirm.

Within a course, you can create smaller groups for the purposes of assignments and reporting. These are called Assignment Groups, and existing student users can be placed in as many of these groups as you like. Note that these groups will only exist within the course that you are in. To create an Assignment Group:

  • click on Group Access to the top left of your screen
  • select the group on the left within which you wish to create the group
  • under the Assignment Groups heading, click Create an Assignment Group
  • give the Group a name
  • select the students who should be in the group from the list, by clicking on the rows
  • click Save, and then Save Changes.

Note that only the Administrator can create student accounts. The student needs to have an account before they can be added to/join a group.

Q: How do I edit a Group?

A: There are three types of group: Global Groups, Code Groups and Assignment Groups.

Global Groups can only be edited or deleted by your Administrator. But as a Teacher user you can add or remove these Groups from your course. In the Group Access window, click ‘Add/Remove Global Groups’, untick the groups you no longer wish to have access, then press Save. Press Save Changes in the main window for the changes to update in the main list.

Code Groups can be edited or deleted by any Teacher with access to that course. In the Group Access window select the Code Group you wish to delete, then under the Access via Group Code heading click Edit Group and its Students. Here you can edit the group name and remove student access to the course by unticking students from the list and clicking Save and then Save Changes in the main window. You may need to refresh the page for the student list to be updated.

Assignment Groups can be edited or deleted by any Teacher with access to that course. In the Group Access window select the Assignment Group you wish to delete, then under the Assignment Groups heading click Edit Group and its Students. Here you can edit the group name and remove student access to the course by unticking students from the list and clicking Save and then Save Changes in the main window. You may need to refresh the page for the student list to be updated.

Q: How do I delete a Group?

A: There are three types of group: Global Groups, Code Groups and Assignment Groups.

Global Groups can only be edited or deleted by your Administrator. But as a Teacher user you can add or remove these Groups from your course. In the Group Access window, click ‘Add/Remove Global Groups’, untick the groups you no longer wish to have access, then press Save. Press Save Changes in the main window for the changes to update in the main list.

Code Groups can be edited or deleted by any Teacher with access to that course. In the Group Access window select the Code Group you wish to delete, then under the Access via Group Code heading click Delete Group. Any students who have signed up to the course using this group will lose access, although any assignments or marks they have will be kept for when/if the student gains access to the course again. Any sub groups within the Code Group will be deleted.

Assignment Groups can be edited or deleted by any Teacher with access to that course. In the Group Access window select the Assignment Group you wish to delete, then under the Assignment Groups heading click Delete Group. Doing this does not remove student access to the course, but any sub groups within the Assignment Group will be deleted.

Q: Can other teachers edit groups I’ve created?

A: Any Teacher within a course can add and remove Global Groups, and edit and delete any Code Groups and Assignment Groups. This means that any changes you make will be automatically updated for all other Teachers with access to that course, as will any changes any other Teacher may make.

Logging in, usernames and passwords

Q: What information is needed to log in?

A:

To login, each user needs a username, password, and the institution code.

Q: I can’t log in - what should I do?

A: Visit our dedicated Logging In help page for support.

Q: How do students find out their login details?

A: Once the Administrator has created student accounts, you can export a list of their usernames. To do this, go to the User Management tab, then click on User Details. Select one group and then click Export. By default student passwords will be the same as their username and the institution code will be the same as that of the Teacher and Administrator.

If you’re looking for an easy way to distribute your students’ login details to your classes, you can use this handy tool on our support site.

Q: Can I export a list of students?

A: Yes, you can. As well as being the best way of finding out your students’ login details, you can also export their email addresses and student ID numbers.

To do this, go to the User Management tab, then click on User Details. Select one group and then click Export.

If you’re looking for an easy way to distribute your students’ login details to your classes, you can use this handy tool on our support site.

Q: My students can’t log in - what can I do?

A: Visit our dedicated Logging In help page for support.

Q: I’ve forgotten my password - what should I do?

A: Visit our dedicated Logging In help page for support.

Q: How are the student logins created?

A: Your system Administrator will create the student logins in bulk, by exporting their names and groups from your institution’s management system.

The default usernames are the first letter of the first name, followed by the surname. For example, Sally Smith will have a username of: ssmith

If two students’ names will create identical usernames, then one of them will have a number automatically added on to the end. Look at their entries in the User Management module to identify these users.

For first login, the student’s password will be the same as their username. The system will force them to change this password when they first log in. It must be 6 characters or more.

The students will also need their institution code. This is the same as yours, and is provided to you at the same time as your login details, or by your system Administrator.

Q: My student has forgotten their password - what can I do?

A:

You can reset their password - this will return it to the default, which is the same as their username. Find the student in the User Management module, using the Search bar, or the Groups filters on the left. Select the student from the list, and click on Preview from above the list. This will open the Preview pane. In it there is an option Reset Password. Click on the tickbox, then click Save Changes at the bottom. The student’s password is now reset.
